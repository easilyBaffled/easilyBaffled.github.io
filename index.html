Waitless is an application designed to take you out of line. By tracking all of its users in line, Waitless does your waiting for you and allows you to roam free. At conventions or theme parks, you can be in line for multiple events at once, while walking around. This application has been designed to be as easy to use as possbile, because convenience shouldn't be a hassle. This is a website devoted to the development of that applcation.

WEEK 2
No Images This Week.

Project Pitch

Initially we each had to come up with a long list of possible ideas to implement over the course the semester. From there we each had to choose two of the best from our lists, and write a short pitch. That pitch would be shown to the class to decide which projects would end up being implemented. We all chose Waitless.

WEEK 5

Formative Research

We had to gather any information we could about lines. It just so happened that the University of Maryland was having its career fair so we chose to study that. We used a survey for the Computer Science Fair, and then personally investigated the Main Career fair. The images are from the large Maryland Carrer fair.

WEEK 5

Proposal and Presentation

The proposal outlined the analysis of our research and the formal description of what we hoped to do. The presentation was a condensed version of that report, which was shown to the class.

WEEK 7

Sketches and Storyboards

For the rest of the project we chose three tasks to focus on, adding a Line, accepting a call to a line, and trading places in lines. We each drew a large number of sketches and storyboards to show all of the different ways that we could implement the interface. There was a lot of experimentation in this section.

WEEK 10

Paper Prototypes

After presenting the sketches and storyboards to our classmates, we were able to choose two designs to focus on. We created two paper prototypes that could simulate each task so that we could test our designs.

WEEK 11

Mid-Fi Prototypes

As a result of the feedback we got from the paper prototypes, the mid-fi prototypes were a cleaner version of the design. This time they were built using wireframing software so their interactions more closely reflected that of the final product.

WEEK 16

Final Prototype, Report, Presentation

This prototype is the result of all of the design and testing we have done. The presentation is a video displaying the prototype in action, and the report will detail how it was designed.

Initial Pitch

Take back our time from line with technology.

As individuals, we have no actual purpose in a line, we really only matter once we get to the front. Everyone might as well just be monopoly pieces, with someone moving us all along. We should be free to roam as we please, and just leave a token in our place, instead of being forced to trudge along in near torturous lines[1]. Restaurants are still trying to find a satisfactory way to seat their customers. They have yet to find a way to deal with amassing lines, while avoiding the no show problem, without frustrating customers at the same time[2]. Amusement parks have adapted fast passes, but they cost an arm and a leg, and are not easily applicable outside of the park[3]. Disney tries to hide their lines and other tricks, so we don't realize how long they actually are[1]. Still no one has found a way make life better for the customer. That is what Waitless is for. Using unique event and user IDs Waitless will keep track of everyones location in every line. Users will be free to wander as they please, and Waitless will call them when they are near the front of a line. The application will analyze and reorganize each line so that everyone gets where they want as efficiently as possible. Waitless will use an easy to scale and deploy system so that it can be applied wherever people are forced to wait. I initially plan to only target people with iPhones, at smaller events like a Maryland Football game, though it should be applicable almost anywhere. I intend to make Waitless easy and cheap to deploy, and while I can truly only declare victory over lines when they are totally eliminated, for initial development I will look to measure free time gained when using the system in small simulations.
